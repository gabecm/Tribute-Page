Eyes Wide Open [10/26/2020]

Here's a timeline of Twices Career so far...

  • February 11, 2015 - Park Jinyoung announced that the line up for their upcoming girl group would be decided through Sixteen, a reality competition survival show.
  • October 20, 2015 - Debuted with a mini album, The Story Begins, and title track Like Ooh-Ahh. The music video became the most-viewed debut music group for any K-pop group at the time amassing 50 million views within 5 months of their debut.
  • April 25, 2016 - Released a second mini album, Page Two, and title track Cheer Up.
  • May 5, 2016 - Won their first music program award on M! Countdown, and this was followed by wins on the Music Bank and Inkigayo.
  • October 24, 2016 - Released another mini album, TWICEcoaster: Lane 1, and title track TT.
  • January 3, 2017 - TT. becomes the fastest K-pop group music video to hit 100 million views at the time. This single is Twice's most viewed music video to date.
  • February 20, 2017 - Released a repackaged album with a new lead single, Knock Knock. This mini album broke Twice's previous record as it sold more than 300,000 copies in pre-orders within a week.
  • May 15, 2017 - Released a mini album Signal with its lead single also called Signal.
  • June 28, 2017 - Officially debut in Japan with a release of their first compilation ablum, #Twice. The album had the highest first week album sales among K-pop groups in Japan and earned a Platnium certification by the Recording Industry Association of Japan (RIAJ).
  • October 18, 2017 - Released first Japanese single, One More Time. The single album had the largest first-day sales and became the fastest selling album for any K-pop girl group in Japan.
  • October 30, 2017 - Released first full-length album, Twicetagram, and title track Likey. The album and lead single debuted at the top of the Billboard's World Albums and World Digital Song Sales making it a first for K-pop girl groups.
